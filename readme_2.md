# Cascading Extremes Transformer — Mathematical Overview

This document summarizes the mathematical model implemented in this repo. It is designed to align Extreme Value Theory (EVT) with a causal transformer that learns and generates cascading extremes as a marked point process.

## 1. Margins and Radial–Angular Representation

Let $\{\mathbf{X}_t\}_{t\ge 0}$ be an $\mathbb{R}_+^d$-valued, $\{\mathcal{F}_t\}$-adapted process with **standard exponential margins**. We write the radial–angular decomposition

$$
R_t = \|\mathbf{X}_t\|, \qquad \mathbf{W}_t = \mathbf{X}_t / R_t \in \mathcal{S}^{d-1}_+,
$$

where $\mathcal{S}^{d-1}_+$ is the positive simplex.

## 2. Extreme Region and Exceedance Measure

Given a time-varying gauge $g_t$, define the extreme region

$$
\mathcal{E}_t = \{(w,r) : r\,g_t(w) > 1\} = [0,\infty]^d \setminus \mathcal{G}_t.
$$

Let $0 < T_1 < T_2 < \cdots$ be the successive hitting times of $\mathcal{E}_t$ and define marks

$$
M_i = (\mathbf{W}_{T_i}, R_{T_i}) \in \mathcal{M} := \mathcal{S}^{d-1}_+ \times (0,\infty).
$$

The exceedances are encoded by the random measure

$$
N = \sum_{i\ge 1} \delta_{(T_i, M_i)}
\quad \text{on } \mathbb{R}_+ \times \mathcal{M}.
$$

Assume the predictable compensator

$$
\Lambda(dt,dm) = \lambda(t,m)\,dt\,\nu(dm),
$$

with an exogenous–endogenous decomposition

$$
\lambda(t,m) = \mu(t,m) + \psi(t,m), \qquad \mu,\psi \ge 0,
$$

where $\psi$ is a predictable functional of the history $\mathcal{H}_t$.

## 3. Cascading Extremes Measure

Define Bernoulli marks $U_i$ by

$$
\mathbb{P}(U_i=1\mid \mathcal{H}_{T_i}) = \frac{\psi(T_i, M_i)}{\lambda(T_i, M_i)}.
$$

Then the cascading extremes measure is

$$
N_{\mathrm{C}}(dt,dm) = \sum_{i\ge1} U_i\,\delta_{(T_i,M_i)}(dt,dm).
$$

The quantity $\psi/\lambda$ is interpreted as the **cascade probability**.

## 4. Direction-Dependent Threshold (Geometric Extremes)

We model a **directional quantile threshold**

$$
R_t > u_\tau(\mathbf{W}_t),
$$

where $u_\tau(\cdot)$ is learned by conditional quantile regression at level $\tau$. The gauge proxy is

$$
 g_0(w) = 1 / u_\tau(w).
$$

## 5. Tokenization and Causal Transformer

For each event $i$ define the token

$$
\Phi_i = \big(\mathbf{W}_{T_i},\, \zeta(\mathbf{W}_{T_i}),\, \log R_{T_i},\, \log \Delta_i\big),
\quad \Delta_i = T_i - T_{i-1}.
$$

A causal transformer maps the past into a latent state

$$
h_i = \mathrm{Transformer}(\Phi_{1:i}).
$$

## 6. Three-Head Generative Model

Given $h_i$, the next event is generated by three heads.

### 6.1 Direction Head (Mixture of Dirichlet)

$$
\mathbf{W}_{T_{i+1}} \mid \mathcal{H}_{T_i} \sim \sum_{k=1}^K \pi_{i,k}\,\mathrm{Dir}(\alpha_{i,k}),
$$

with

$$
\pi_i = \mathrm{softmax}(\Pi(h_i)), \quad
\alpha_{i,k} = \mathrm{softplus}(A_k(h_i)).
$$

### 6.2 Gauge / Magnitude Head (Truncated Gamma)

The rate is

$$
\beta_{i+1} = g_{T_i}(\mathbf{W}_{T_{i+1}})
= \mathrm{softplus}(\Upsilon(h_i, \log R_{T_i}, \mathbf{W}_{T_{i+1}})).
$$

Then

$$
R_{T_{i+1}} \mid \{\mathbf{W}_{T_{i+1}}, R_{T_{i+1}}>u\} \sim \mathrm{truncGamma}(a, \beta_{i+1}).
$$

### 6.3 Time Head (Hawkes Feedback)

$$
\lambda_i = \mu(h_i) + \sum_{j\le i} \kappa(T_i - T_j, h_i, h_j)\,\varphi(R_{T_j}),
$$

with

$$
\mu(h_i) = \mathrm{softplus}(\mu_\theta(h_i)),
$$

$$
\kappa(\Delta_{ij},h_i,h_j) = \mathrm{softplus}(k_\theta([h_i,h_j]))\,\exp\!\Big(-\frac{\Delta_{ij}}{\mathrm{softplus}(\tau_\theta([h_i,h_j]))}\Big),
$$

and $\varphi(\cdot)$ a learned positive MLP. The inter-event time distribution is

$$
\Delta_{i+1} \sim \mathrm{Exp}(\lambda_i),
$$

with log-likelihood

$$
\log p(\Delta_{i+1} \mid h_i) = \log \lambda_i - \lambda_i \Delta_{i+1}.
$$

## 7. Likelihood Objective

The training objective maximizes the joint log-likelihood

$$
\mathcal{L} = \sum_i \big( \log p(\mathbf{W}_{i+1}\mid h_i) + \log p(R_{i+1}\mid h_i) + \log p(\Delta_{i+1}\mid h_i) \big),
$$

with mild regularization on Hawkes parameters for stability.

## 8. Simulation

Given a seed history, simulate sequentially:

1. $\Delta_{i+1} \sim \mathrm{Exp}(\lambda_i)$
2. $\mathbf{W}_{i+1} \sim \sum_k \pi_{i,k}\,\mathrm{Dir}(\alpha_{i,k})$
3. $R_{i+1} \sim \mathrm{truncGamma}(a, \beta_{i+1})$

This yields a synthetic cascade in $(T_i, \mathbf{W}_i, R_i)$ space.

## 9. Data Standardization (Crypto Returns)

We take log-returns per asset, fit GARCH(1,1) with Student-$t$ innovations, and standardize residuals $z_t^j$. An empirical CDF $\hat F_j$ yields standard exponential margins via the PIT:

$$
Y_t^j = -\log(1-\hat F_j(z_t^j)),
$$

and define $\mathbf{X}_t = (Y_t^1,\ldots,Y_t^d)$.

---

This formalism connects EVT geometry with causal sequence modeling and provides a principled generative model for cascading extremes.
